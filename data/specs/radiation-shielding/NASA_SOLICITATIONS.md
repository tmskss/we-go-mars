# NASA Radiation Shielding Solicitations: Legacy and Current Opportunities

This dossier summarizes NASA solicitations and funded projects focused on radiation shielding for human exploration missions. It draws from NASA procurement archives, SBIR/STTR databases, NextSTEP partnerships, and Human Research Program (HRP) investments up to late 2024. Budget figures are indicative and subject to official NASA documentation.

## 1. NASA Procurement Pathways Relevant to Shielding

- **Next Space Technologies for Exploration Partnerships (NextSTEP):** Public-private partnerships (contracts/cooperative agreements) supporting habitation, life support, and advanced materials. Awards typically range $8–$65M with multi-year performance.
- **Human Research Program (HRP):** Supports biomedical and radiation research via grants, NRA solicitations, and Directed Research Tasks; budgets from $0.5–$5M per multi-year award.
- **Space Technology Mission Directorate (STMD) Programs:**
  - *Game Changing Development (GCD):* Mid-TRL technology maturation.
  - *Technology Demonstration Missions (TDM):* Flight demonstrations.
  - *Small Business Innovation Research (SBIR) / Small Business Technology Transfer (STTR):* Phase I (~$150k) and Phase II (~$750k) awards for SMEs.
- **Radiation Biophysics Program / RadWorks (Johnson Space Center):** Focused instrument development and dosimetry modernization.
- **Agency Announcements (BAA/AO):** Broad agency announcements for specific mission support (e.g., Artemis, Gateway).

## 2. Selected Completed Efforts

### 2.1 NextSTEP Habitat Radiation Studies (2015–2018)

- **Solicitation:** NextSTEP Broad Agency Announcement Appendix A (NNJ15ZSA001N).
- **Awardees:** Bigelow Aerospace, Boeing, Lockheed Martin, Orbital ATK, Sierra Nevada Corporation, NanoRacks, Dynetics.
- **Radiation Objectives:** Each partner delivered habitat concepts featuring integrated radiation protection (water walls, storm shelters, logistics mass). Analyses used NASA’s HZETRN/OCT and OLTARIS tools.
- **Outcomes:**
  - Lockheed Martin’s Deep Space Habitat design integrated multi-purpose logistics and consumables for shielding, informing Gateway HALO storm shelter placement.
  - Bigelow Aerospace’s expandable habitat included water-lined sleeping quarters; data fed into BEAM ISS demonstration.
  - Resulted in 2018 NASA report “NextSTEP Phase 2 Habitat Concepts” summarizing shielding mass distributions and compliance strategies.

### 2.2 RadWorks Hybrid Electronic Radiation Assessor (HERA)

- **Program:** NASA RadWorks (Johnson Space Center).
- **Contracts:** Multiple task orders (2014–2020) with Leidos, Lockheed Martin, and JSC engineering directorate.
- **Scope:** Develop Orion’s primary radiation monitoring system integrating silicon detectors, plastic scintillators, and real-time processing.
- **Results:** HERA achieved TRL 7 during Artemis I (2022), providing continuous dose-rate monitoring and automated caution/warning integration.

### 2.3 HRP Omics and Dosimetry Integration

- **Solicitation:** NASA Research Announcement (NRA) NNH16ZTT001N (Human Exploration Research Opportunities).
- **Project Example:** “Integrated Biophysical Models of Space Radiation Effects” (PI: F. Cucinotta).
- **Achievements:** Developed human exposure models linking dosimetry data with biological responses, underpinning career dose limit assessments and shielding trade studies.

### 2.4 SBIR/STTR Successes

- **Phase II Example:** “Advanced Hydrogenated Boron Nitride Nanotube Composites for Radiation Shielding” (NNX17CM19C, 2017–2019) by Nanocomp Technologies.
  - Demonstrated structural composites with high hydrogen content, achieving >30% dose-equivalent reduction versus aluminum.
  - Transitioned to STMD follow-on for scale-up testing.
- **Phase II Example:** “Active Space Radiation Shield for Deep-Space Missions” (NNX17CR72C) by Physical Optics Corporation.
  - Developed superconducting magnetic shield concepts with integrated power and thermal management models.
  - Provided feasibility data for long-term active shielding research roadmaps.

### 2.5 Space Radiation Analysis Group (SRAG) Directed Studies

- **Internal Work:** SRAG at JSC runs continuous modeling to update Design Reference Mission (DRM) environments.
- **Key Deliverables:** 2019 “Artemis Radiation Protection Guidelines” and 2023 “Orion Deep Space Radiation Analysis” reports, which feed into mission shielding plans and risk assessments.

## 3. Notable Historical Research Programmes

- **Bioastronautics Initiative (2005–2010):** Early convergence of life sciences, shielding, and mission design; produced foundational data on passive materials and operational constraints.
- **Exploration Systems Mission Directorate (ESMD) Habitation Projects (2007–2012):** Funded shielding studies for Altair lunar lander and Deep Space Habitat prototypes; results archived in NASA Technical Reports Server (NTRS).

## 4. Current and Recent Solicitations (2023–2024)

### 4.1 Human Exploration Research Opportunities (HERO) NRA – Appendix D: Space Radiobiology

- **Reference:** NNH24ZTT001N-D (released June 2024).
- **Focus Areas:**
  - Radioprotective pharmaceuticals and dietary countermeasures.
  - Omics-informed risk models for chronic low-dose-rate exposure.
  - Advanced organ-on-a-chip platforms for radiation studies.
- **Funding Profile:** Up to $1M per award over 4 years.
- **Schedule:** Step-2 proposals due November 2024; selections expected Q2 2025.

### 4.2 STMD GCD Call for Proposals: Multifunctional Habitat Structures

- **Reference:** GCD-2024-02.
- **Objective:** Develop structural panels integrating thermal, micrometeoroid, and radiation shielding functions for Mars transit habitats.
- **Expected Deliverables:** Full-scale panel prototype, coupled thermal/radiation analysis, and test campaign plan (NSRL exposure).
- **Award Range:** $5–10M cooperative agreements; awards anticipated late 2024 with 36-month period.

### 4.3 SBIR Phase I 2024 Topic Z10.07 – Novel Materials for Space Radiation Mitigation

- **Solicitation:** 2024 SBIR/STTR Phase I (NNH24ZSB001N).
- **Topic Goals:** Hydrogen-rich composites, regolith-derived materials, adaptive shielding concepts.
- **Phase I Outcomes:** Up to $150k for 6-month feasibility; successful candidates invited to Phase II in 2025.

### 4.4 NextSTEP Appendix P – Mars Transit Habitat Risk Reduction

- **Status:** Anticipated release (draft RFI issued 2023).
- **Scope:** Industry partnerships to mature Mars transit habitat subsystems, including radiation protection, dosimetry integration, and operations.
- **Aims:** Deliver subsystem prototypes and integrated analysis demonstrating compliance with 600 mSv career limit under specified DRMs.
- **Timeline:** Final solicitation expected FY2025 pending budget authorization.

### 4.5 RadWorks Technology Development Tasks (FY2024–2026)

- **Planned Activities:** Upgrades to HERA, development of distributed dosimetry sensor networks, and miniaturized Medipix-based personal dosimeters.
- **Procurement:** Task orders via the JSC Engineering, Technology and Science (JETS) contract (Jacobs/Leidos). Opportunities typically advertised via SAM.gov task order notices.

## 5. Emerging Interests and Trends

- **Integration with Digital Twins:** NASA emphasizes coupling shielding design with mission digital twins; solicitations often seek software tool enhancements (HZETRN, OLTARIS).
- **Public-Private Collaboration:** NextSTEP and follow-on partnerships combine agency requirements with commercial transport concepts (e.g., SpaceX Starship integration studies).
- **Biology-Driven Requirements:** HERO solicitations increasingly tie shielding decisions to biological markers (omics, immune response), indicating a convergence of physical and biological mitigation.
- **Operational Demonstrations:** Upcoming Artemis missions serve as testbeds; solicitations prioritize technologies that can fly on Gateway, Orion, or lunar surface missions before Mars.

4. **Industry Days:** Attend NASA Deep Space Logistics and Exploration forums where upcoming solicitations are previewed.
5. **RadWorks & SRAG Workshops:** Annual meetings provide insight into near-term priorities and technology gaps.
