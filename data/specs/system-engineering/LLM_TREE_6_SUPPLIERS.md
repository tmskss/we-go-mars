# LLM Prompt Tree – Module 6: Suppliers, Software & Policy `[P2|MS]`

## 6. Supplier and Industrial Ecosystem

- **6.1 Materials Suppliers**
  - 6.1.1 Which SMEs produce hydrogen-rich composites with space heritage?
    - 6.1.1.1 What certifications or flight heritage (ISS, Artemis) do they hold?
      - 6.1.1.1.1 How recent are qualification tests and what standards were used?
      - 6.1.1.1.2 Do certifications cover crewed-mission safety requirements?
    - 6.1.1.2 What is their annual production capacity and typical lead time?
    - 6.1.1.3 How flexible are they in customizing formulations or geometries?
  - 6.1.2 What lead times and production scales can suppliers commit to?
    - 6.1.2.1 How do order volumes impact schedule and cost?
    - 6.1.2.2 What supply chain risks (feedstock availability) exist?
    - 6.1.2.3 How can redundancy be maintained across multiple suppliers?
  - 6.1.3 How do regional export controls affect material availability?
    - 6.1.3.1 Which materials fall under ITAR, EAR, or EU dual-use regulations?
    - 6.1.3.2 How can international partnerships navigate licensing requirements?
    - 6.1.3.3 What local alternatives exist if exports are restricted?

- **6.2 Instrumentation Providers**
  - 6.2.1 Which companies deliver radiation spectrometers compatible with crew habitats?
    - 6.2.1.1 What sensitivity ranges and energy resolution do they offer?
    - 6.2.1.2 How ruggedized are systems for dust and thermal extremes?
    - 6.2.1.3 What telemetry interfaces and power requirements do they have?
  - 6.2.2 What calibration services are necessary for mission readiness?
    - 6.2.2.1 Which vendors provide calibration under space-like conditions?
    - 6.2.2.2 How often must recalibration occur during long missions?
    - 6.2.2.3 What documentation supports certification audits?
  - 6.2.3 How can small models summarize supplier capabilities from procurement data?
    - 6.2.3.1 What public datasets (SBIR, ESA-Star) should be monitored?
    - 6.2.3.2 How can NLP extract key attributes (TRL, heritage, capacity)?
    - 6.2.3.3 How are false positives filtered to maintain accuracy?

- **6.3 Software and Analytics**
  - 6.3.1 Which SMEs offer radiation transport or forecasting services?
    - 6.3.1.1 What proprietary codes or model wrappers do they provide?
    - 6.3.1.2 How are services delivered (SaaS platforms, consulting)?
    - 6.3.1.3 What validation evidence supports their tools?
  - 6.3.2 How can contracts (NASA SBIR, ESA GSTP) be tracked for emerging tools?
    - 6.3.2.1 What portals and RSS feeds publish award notices?
    - 6.3.2.2 How can LLMs auto-summarize contract scopes and deliverables?
    - 6.3.2.3 How are technology readiness updates captured over time?
  - 6.3.3 What open-source datasets can small models ingest to maintain supplier profiles?
    - 6.3.3.1 Which datasets (NASA TechPort, EU Cordis) contain relevant info?
    - 6.3.3.2 How can data be normalized for cross-region comparison?
    - 6.3.3.3 How are profile updates validated against official sources?

- **6.4 Procurement and Policy**
  - 6.4.1 What funding programs (NASA SBIR, ESA GSTP, JAXA RA, ISRO RESPOND) support shielding innovation?
    - 6.4.1.1 What eligibility criteria and funding levels apply to each program?
    - 6.4.1.2 How can proposals align with current agency priorities?
    - 6.4.1.3 What timelines and reporting requirements must be managed?
  - 6.4.2 How do export controls (ITAR, EAR, EU dual-use) impact international collaboration?
    - 6.4.2.1 What licensing processes are needed for cross-border sharing?
    - 6.4.2.2 How can projects segregate controlled information?
    - 6.4.2.3 What precedents exist for multinational shielding collaborations?
  - 6.4.3 How can LLMs monitor tender portals (ESA-Star, SAM.gov) for relevant solicitations?
    - 6.4.3.1 What scraping or API approaches respect portal terms?
    - 6.4.3.2 How can alerts be categorized by technology or TRL?
    - 6.4.3.3 What manual reviews ensure relevance and accuracy?
  - 6.4.4 What certification standards (NASA’s FOD, ECSS-Q) must suppliers meet?
    - 6.4.4.1 What documentation (quality plans, process controls) is required?
    - 6.4.4.2 How are audits conducted for small suppliers?
    - 6.4.4.3 How can suppliers prepare for crew safety qualification?
  - 6.4.5 How are supplier risks (schedule, quality) tracked in program management tools?
    - 6.4.5.1 What KPIs monitor supplier performance?
    - 6.4.5.2 How are risk registers integrated with procurement systems?
    - 6.4.5.3 How can LLMs summarize supplier status for program reviews?
